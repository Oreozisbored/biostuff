## 📖 Chapter Summary
This chapter introduces the fundamental concepts of scientific inquiry, highlighting how researchers make observations, form hypotheses, design controlled experiments with specific variables, and analyze qualitative and quantitative data. It also covers scientific reasoning, microscopy, and the distinction between hypothesis and theory, culminating in peer review.
