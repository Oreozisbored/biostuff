## 📘 COMPREHENSIVE STUDY GUIDE & ANSWERS

### Part 1: Chapter Objectives Explanations

#### 3.1 Carbon Bonding Properties
*   Carbon has 4 valence electrons in an outer shell that holds 8.
*   To fill its shell, carbon completes its valence shell by sharing electrons with other atoms in four covalent bonds.
*   This tetravalence allows carbon to build large, complex, and diverse molecules. Carbon chains form the backbone (skeleton) of organic molecules. Skeletons can be straight, branched, or arranged in rings.

#### 3.2 Functional Groups
*   **Functional Groups**: Specific groups of atoms attached to carbon skeletons that participate in chemical reactions and give organic molecules their unique properties. They are hydrophilic and increase solubility in water.
    *   **Hydroxyl Group** ($-OH$): Found in alcohols and sugars.
    *   **Carbonyl Group** ($>C=O$): Found in sugars.
    *   **Carboxyl Group** ($-COOH$): Acts as an acid by donating $H^+$; found in amino acids.
    *   **Amino Group** ($-NH_2$): Acts as a base by picking up $H^+$; found in amino acids.
    *   **Phosphate Group** ($-OPO_3^{2-}$): Involved in energy transfer (ATP).
    *   **Methyl Group** ($-CH_3$): Nonpolar; affects gene expression when added to DNA.

#### 3.3 Macromolecules, Monomers, and Polymers
*   **Macromolecules**: Giant molecules formed by joining smaller molecules. The four main classes are carbohydrates, lipids, proteins, and nucleic acids.
*   **Polymer**: A long molecule consisting of many identical or similar building blocks linked by covalent bonds.
*   **Monomer**: The subunit that serves as the building block of a polymer.
*   **Dehydration Reaction**: A chemical reaction in which two monomers bond together with the removal of a water molecule. Builds polymers.
*   **Hydrolysis**: A chemical reaction that breaks bonds between monomers by adding a water molecule. Breaks down polymers.

#### 3.4–3.7 Carbohydrates
*   **Monosaccharides**: Single sugars, the monomers of carbohydrates (e.g., Glucose, Fructose). Have molecular formulas that are multiples of $CH_2O$. Main fuel for cellular work.
*   **Disaccharides**: Two monosaccharides linked by a dehydration reaction (e.g., Sucrose = Glucose + Fructose, Lactose, Maltose).
*   **Polysaccharides**: Polymers of hundreds to thousands of monosaccharides.
    *   **Starch**: Storage polysaccharide in plants made of glucose monomers.
    *   **Glycogen**: Storage polysaccharide in animals, highly branched, stored in liver and muscle cells.
    *   **Cellulose**: Structural polysaccharide in plant cell walls. Rigid structure cannot be digested by humans (insoluble fiber).
    *   **Chitin**: Structural polysaccharide found in fungal cell walls and insect/crustacean exoskeletons.

#### 3.8–3.10 Lipids
*   **Lipids**: A diverse group of hydrophobic molecules. Not true polymers.
*   **Fats (Triglycerides)**: Composed of one glycerol linked to three fatty acids. Main function is long-term energy storage.
    *   **Saturated Fatty Acids**: Contain only single bonds between carbons. Fully saturated with hydrogen. Straight chains, solid at room temperature (e.g., animal fats).
    *   **Unsaturated Fatty Acids**: Contain one or more double bonds between carbons. This creates kinks in the chains, preventing tight packing. Liquid at room temperature (e.g., plant oils).
*   **Phospholipids**: Composed of glycerol linked to a negatively charged phosphate group (hydrophilic head) and two fatty acid tails (hydrophobic). Major component of cell membranes.
*   **Steroids**: Lipids with carbon skeletons bent into four fused rings (e.g., Cholesterol, testosterone, estrogen).

#### 3.11–3.14 Proteins
*   **Proteins**: Polymers of amino acids. Perform structural, catalytic, transport, signaling, and defensive functions.
*   **Amino Acid Structure**: Composed of a central carbon linked to a hydrogen, an amino group, a carboxyl group, and a variable chemical group (**R group / side chain**). There are 20 different amino acids.
*   **Peptide Bond**: The covalent bond formed between two amino acids by a dehydration reaction.
*   **Four Levels of Protein Structure**:
    1.  **Primary Structure**: The unique, linear sequence of amino acids in a polypeptide chain. Determined by genetic information.
    2.  **Secondary Structure**: Coiling ($lpha$-helix) or folding ($eta$-pleated sheet) of the polypeptide chain, stabilized by hydrogen bonds between the polypeptide backbone.
    3.  **Tertiary Structure**: The overall three-dimensional shape of the polypeptide, determined by interactions among R groups (hydrophobic interactions, hydrogen bonds, ionic bonds, disulfide bridges).
    4.  **Quaternary Structure**: The structure resulting from the association of two or more polypeptide subunits (e.g., Hemoglobin, Collagen).
*   **Denaturation**: A process in which a protein unravels and loses its native shape due to changes in pH, salt concentration, or high temperature, making it biologically inactive.

#### 3.15–3.16 Nucleic Acids
*   **Nucleic Acids**: DNA (deoxyribonucleic acid) and RNA (ribonucleic acid).
*   **Monomer**: **Nucleotide**, composed of:
    1.  A five-carbon sugar (deoxyribose in DNA; ribose in RNA).
    2.  A phosphate group.
    3.  A nitrogenous base (Adenine, Thymine, Cytosine, Guanine in DNA; Uracil replaces Thymine in RNA).
*   **Structure**: DNA is double-stranded, forming a double helix held together by hydrogen bonds between complementary bases (A-T, G-C). RNA is single-stranded.

### Part 2: Key Terms Definitions

*   **Amino Acid**: An organic molecule containing a carboxyl group and an amino group; serves as the monomer of proteins.
*   **Carbohydrates**: A class of biological molecules consisting of single-ring sugars, double-ring sugars, and starch/cellulose polymers.
*   **Carbonyl Group**: A chemical group consisting of a carbon atom linked by a double bond to an oxygen atom.
*   **Carboxyl Group**: A chemical group consisting of a carbon atom double-bonded to an oxygen atom and also bonded to a hydroxyl group.
*   **Cellulose**: A structural polysaccharide of plant cell walls, composed of glucose monomers.
*   **Chitin**: A structural polysaccharide found in fungal cell walls and insect exoskeletons.
*   **Cholesterol**: A steroid that is an important component of animal cell membranes and acts as a precursor for other steroids.
*   **Dehydration Reaction**: A chemical reaction in which two molecules become covalently bonded with the removal of a water molecule.
*   **Denaturation**: A process in which a protein loses its specific structure, and consequently its function, due to environmental disruption.
*   **Disaccharide**: A sugar molecule consisting of two monosaccharides linked by a dehydration reaction.
*   **Double Helix**: The form of native DNA, referring to its two adjacent polynucleotide strands wound into a spiral shape.
*   **Fat**: A lipid composed of three fatty acids linked to one glycerol molecule; a triglyceride.
*   **Functional Group**: An assemblage of atoms commonly attached to the carbon skeletons of organic molecules and involved in chemical reactions.
*   **Glycogen**: An extensively branched glucose storage polysaccharide found in liver and muscle cells of animals.
*   **Hydrolysis**: A chemical reaction that breaks bonds between two molecules by the addition of water.
*   **Hydrophilic**: "Water-loving"; pertaining to polar or charged molecules that dissolve in water.
*   **Hydrophobic**: "Water-fearing"; pertaining to nonpolar molecules that do not dissolve in water.
*   **Hydroxyl Group**: A chemical group consisting of an oxygen atom bonded to a hydrogen atom.
*   **Lipids**: An organic compound consisting mainly of carbon and hydrogen atoms linked by nonpolar covalent bonds, making it hydrophobic.
*   **Macromolecule**: A giant molecule in a living organism (protein, carbohydrate, nucleic acid).
*   **Monomer**: The subunit that serves as the building block of a polymer.
*   **Monosaccharide**: The simplest carbohydrate; a simple sugar.
*   **Nucleic Acid**: A polymer consisting of many nucleotide monomers; serves as a blueprint for proteins (DNA and RNA).
*   **Nucleotide**: The monomer of nucleic acids, consisting of a five-carbon sugar, a phosphate group, and a nitrogenous base.
*   **Organic Compounds**: Chemical compounds containing the element carbon and usually synthesized by cells.
*   **Peptide Bond**: The covalent bond between two amino acid units in a polypeptide, formed by a dehydration reaction.
*   **Phospholipid**: A lipid made up of glycerol joined to two fatty acids and a phosphate group, forming bilayers in cell membranes.
*   **Polymer**: A large molecule consisting of many identical or similar monomers linked together by covalent bonds.
*   **Polypeptide**: A polymer of amino acids linked by peptide bonds.
*   **Polysaccharide**: A carbohydrate polymer of many monosaccharides linked by dehydration reactions.
*   **Protein**: A functional biological macromolecule consisting of one or more polypeptide chains folded into a specific 3D shape.
*   **RNA (Ribonucleic Acid)**: A type of nucleic acid consisting of nucleotide monomers with a ribose sugar and nitrogenous bases A, U, C, G; usually single-stranded.
*   **Saturated Fatty Acid**: A fatty acid in which all carbons in the hydrocarbon tail are connected by single bonds, maximizing hydrogen content.
*   **Starch**: A storage polysaccharide in plants, consisting entirely of glucose.
*   **Steroid**: A type of lipid whose carbon skeleton is in the form of four fused rings.
*   **Triglyceride**: Synonymous with fat; composed of three fatty acids and one glycerol.
*   **Unsaturated Fatty Acid**: A fatty acid that has one or more double bonds between carbon atoms in the hydrocarbon tail, reducing hydrogen content.
