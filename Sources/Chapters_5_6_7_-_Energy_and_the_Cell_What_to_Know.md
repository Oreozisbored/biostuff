### Part 1: Chapter Objectives Explanations

#### 5.10–5.13 Energy and ATP
*   **Kinetic Energy**: Energy of motion (e.g., heat, light).
*   **Potential Energy**: Stored energy due to position or chemical structure (e.g., chemical energy stored in bonds of molecules like glucose).
*   **First Law of Thermodynamics**: Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transferred or transformed.
*   **Second Law of Thermodynamics**: Every energy transfer or transformation increases the entropy (disorder) of the universe. Some energy is always lost as heat.
*   **ATP (Adenosine Triphosphate)**: The cell's energy currency. Composed of ribose, adenine, and three phosphate groups. The bonds between phosphate groups are unstable and rich in potential energy. Hydrolysis of the terminal phosphate releases energy, forming **ADP** and inorganic phosphate ($P_i$). The cell drives endergonic (energy-requiring) reactions by coupling them to the exergonic (energy-releasing) hydrolysis of ATP.

#### 5.14–5.16 Enzymes and Activation Energy
*   **Enzymes**: Biological catalysts, mostly proteins, that speed up chemical reactions by lowering the **activation energy** ($E_A$) barrier. They do NOT change the free energy ($\Delta G$) of the reaction.
*   **Substrate**: The specific reactant an enzyme acts on.
*   **Active Site**: The pocket or groove on the enzyme where the substrate binds.
*   **Induced Fit**: The slight change in shape of the active site as the substrate binds, securing it in place and facilitating the reaction.
*   **Factors Affecting Enzymes**:
    *   *Temperature and pH*: Each enzyme has an *optimal* temperature and pH at which it operates fastest. Extreme conditions lead to **denaturation** (loss of 3D shape and function).
*   **Enzyme Inhibition**:
    *   **Competitive Inhibitor**: A substance that reduces enzyme activity by entering the active site in place of the substrate, mimicking its shape. Can be overcome by increasing substrate concentration.
    *   **Noncompetitive Inhibitor**: Binds to another part of the enzyme (allosteric site), changing the shape of the active site so that the substrate can no longer bind or be processed.

#### 6.1–6.12 Cellular Respiration
*   **Cellular Respiration**: The aerobic harvesting of chemical energy from organic fuel molecules (glucose) to synthesize ATP. Overall equation:
    $$	ext{C}_6	ext{H}_{12}	ext{O}_6 + 6	ext{O}_2 ightarrow 6	ext{CO}_2 + 6	ext{H}_2	ext{O} + 	ext{ATP} + 	ext{Heat}$$
*   **Redox Reactions**: Chemical reactions involving electron transfers. Oxidation is loss of electrons; reduction is gain of electrons (OIL RIG). Glucose is oxidized ($CO_2$), and oxygen is reduced ($H_2O$).
*   **Coenzymes**: **$	ext{NAD}^+$** and **$	ext{FAD}$** act as electron carriers, picking up electrons and hydrogen ions to become $	ext{NADH}$ and $	ext{FADH}_2$.
*   **Three Stages of Cellular Respiration**:
    1.  **Glycolysis**: Occurs in the cytoplasm. Splits 6-carbon glucose into two 3-carbon molecules of **pyruvate**. Yields a net of $2\ 	ext{ATP}$ (via substrate-level phosphorylation) and $2\ 	ext{NADH}$. Does not require oxygen.
    2.  **Pyruvate Oxidation and the Krebs (Citric Acid) Cycle**: Occurs in the mitochondrial matrix. Pyruvate is oxidized to Acetyl-CoA, releasing $CO_2$. The Krebs cycle processes Acetyl-CoA, yielding $2\ 	ext{ATP}$ per glucose, and loads electron carriers, yielding $6\ 	ext{NADH}$ and $2\ 	ext{FADH}_2$, while releasing the remaining carbons as $CO_2$.
    3.  **Oxidative Phosphorylation (Electron Transport Chain & Chemiosmosis)**: Occurs on the inner mitochondrial membrane (cristae). 
        *   **Electron Transport Chain (ETC)**: Electrons from $	ext{NADH}$ and $	ext{FADH}_2$ flow down protein complexes to oxygen (the final electron acceptor), which combines with $H^+$ to form $H_2O$. This flow drives the active transport of $H^+$ from the matrix into the intermembrane space, building an $H^+$ gradient.
        *   **Chemiosmosis**: $H^+$ ions flow back down their concentration gradient through **ATP Synthase**, a molecular turbine that drives the phosphorylation of ADP to produce approximately $28\ 	ext{ATP}$.
*   **Anaerobic Respiration / Fermentation**: Regenerates $	ext{NAD}^+$ in the absence of oxygen to keep glycolysis running.
    *   *Lactic Acid Fermentation*: Pyruvate is reduced directly by $	ext{NADH}$ to form lactic acid (e.g., in muscle cells).
    *   *Alcohol Fermentation*: Pyruvate is converted to $CO_2$ and ethanol (e.g., in yeast).

#### 7.1–7.11 Photosynthesis
*   **Photosynthesis**: The process by which plants, algae, and some bacteria convert light energy into chemical energy (sugars). Equation:
    $$6	ext{CO}_2 + 6	ext{H}_2	ext{O} + 	ext{Light} ightarrow 	ext{C}_6	ext{H}_{12}	ext{O}_6 + 6	ext{O}_2$$
*   **Chloroplast Structure**: Inside leaf cells. Contain double membranes, fluid stroma, and stacks of disk-like membranes called **thylakoids** containing the pigment **chlorophyll**.
*   **Two Stages of Photosynthesis**:
    1.  **Light-Dependent Reactions**: Occur in the thylakoid membranes. Chlorophyll in **Photosystems II and I** absorbs solar energy, exciting electrons. 
        *   Water is split to replace lost electrons, releasing oxygen ($O_2$) gas.
        *   Excited electrons flow down an electron transport chain, creating an $H^+$ gradient that drives ATP synthesis via chemiosmosis.
        *   Electrons are finally passed to $	ext{NADP}^+$ to form $	ext{NADPH}$ (electron carrier).
        *   *Products*: Oxygen, ATP, and NADPH.
    2.  **Light-Independent Reactions (Calvin Cycle)**: Occurs in the stroma. Carbon dioxide ($CO_2$) is fixed into organic molecules using the enzyme **RuBisCO**, and reduced into G3P (a 3-carbon sugar used to make glucose) using energy from ATP and electrons from NADPH.

### Part 2: Key Terms Definitions

*   **Activation Energy**: The amount of energy that reactants must absorb before a chemical reaction will start.
*   **Active Site**: The part of an enzyme molecule where a substrate attaches.
*   **ADP (Adenosine Diphosphate)**: A molecule that can be converted into ATP by the addition of a phosphate group.
*   **Aerobic**: Requiring molecular oxygen ($O_2$).
*   **Anaerobic**: Lacking or not requiring molecular oxygen ($O_2$).
*   **ATP (Adenosine Triphosphate)**: The main energy source for cells, consisting of nitrogenous base adenine, ribose sugar, and three phosphate groups.
*   **ATP Synthase**: A cluster of several membrane proteins that function in chemiosmosis with adjacent electron transport chains, using energy of a hydrogen ion gradient to make ATP.
*   **Calvin Cycle**: The second of two stages of photosynthesis; a cyclic series of chemical reactions that occurs in the stroma of a chloroplast, using carbon in $CO_2$ and ATP/NADPH to make the energy-rich sugar G3P.
*   **Catalyst**: A chemical agent that selectively increases the rate of a reaction without being consumed by the reaction.
*   **Cellular Respiration**: The aerobic harvesting of energy from food molecules.
*   **Chemiosmosis**: An energy-coupling mechanism that uses energy stored in the form of a hydrogen ion gradient across a membrane to drive cellular work, such as the synthesis of ATP.
*   **Chlorophyll**: A green pigment located within the chloroplasts of plants, algae, and microscopic cyanobacteria.
*   **Electron Transport Chain**: A sequence of electron carrier proteins (membrane proteins) that shuttle electrons during the redox reactions that release energy used to make ATP.
*   **Entropy**: A measure of disorder or randomness.
*   **Enzyme**: A macromolecule, usually a protein, that serves as a biological catalyst, changing the rate of a chemical reaction without being consumed.
*   **Fermentation**: The anaerobic harvesting of energy from organic food molecules, utilizing glycolysis and regenerating $	ext{NAD}^+$ without an electron transport chain.
*   **Glycolysis**: A series of reactions that ultimately splits glucose into two molecules of pyruvate; the first stage of cellular respiration in all organisms.
*   **Krebs Cycle (Citric Acid Cycle)**: The chemical cycle that completes the metabolic breakdown of glucose molecules begun in glycolysis by oxidizing Acetyl-CoA to $CO_2$.
*   **Light-Dependent Reactions**: The first stage of photosynthesis, converting solar energy to chemical energy (ATP/NADPH) while releasing oxygen.
*   **Light-Independent Reactions**: The second stage of photosynthesis (Calvin Cycle), using ATP and NADPH to fix $CO_2$ into sugars.
*   **NADH / NADPH**: High-energy electron carriers in cellular respiration and photosynthesis, respectively.
*   **Photosynthesis**: The process by which plants, algae, and photosynthetic bacteria use light energy to make organic compounds.
*   **Photosystem**: A light-capturing unit of a chloroplast's thylakoid membrane, consisting of a reaction-center complex surrounded by numerous light-harvesting complexes.
*   **Redox Reaction**: Short for reduction-oxidation reaction; a chemical reaction in which electrons are lost from one substance (oxidation) and added to another (reduction).
*   **Substrate**: A specific reactant on which an enzyme acts.
*   **Thylakoid**: A flattened membranous sac inside a chloroplast, containing chlorophyll.
