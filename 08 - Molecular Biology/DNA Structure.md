# DNA Structure

## Overview
Described by Watson and Crick (using Franklin's X-ray data) in 1953.

DNA forms a double helix composed of two antiparallel strands (running 5' to 3' in opposite directions). The outside is a [[Sugar-Phosphate Backbone]], and the inside consists of paired [[Nitrogenous Bases]] held together by hydrogen bonds following [[Base Pairing Rules]].
