# 🧬 Biology Master Hub & Start Page

Welcome to your ultimate **Biology Study Vault**! This dashboard serves as the central hub for your biology study materials, designed to prepare you for your finals. It connects the **Maps of Content (MOCs)** and **Lesson/Teaching Pages** across 16 core biological topics.

---

## 🧭 Unit-by-Unit Navigation

This vault is organized into five major thematic units. Each link below leads to a **Map of Content (MOC)** that indexes all key concepts, presents a **Chapter Summary**, and links to a comprehensive **Lesson Page** that teaches these concepts.

### 🔬 Unit 1: Foundations of Life
*Scientific inquiry, basic chemistry, and the chemical building blocks of living organisms.*
- 🧪 **[[01 - Scientific Method MOC|Chapter 1: Scientific Method MOC]]** — Hypothesis testing, controlled experiments, variables, and scientific reasoning.
- ⚛️ **[[02 - Chemistry of Life MOC|Chapter 2: Chemistry of Life MOC]]** — Water chemistry, pH, biological macromolecules (proteins, carbs, lipids, nucleic acids), and enzyme function.

### 🧫 Unit 2: Cell Biology & Energetics
*The structural units of life, membrane transport, and how cells capture, store, and utilize energy.*
- 🏥 **[[03 - Cell Structure MOC|Chapter 3: Cell Structure MOC]]** — Organelles, cell theory, endosymbiotic theory, and prokaryotic vs. eukaryotic cell structure.
- 🔄 **[[04 - Membrane and Transport MOC|Chapter 4: Membrane and Transport MOC]]** — Phospholipid bilayer, passive transport (diffusion, osmosis), active transport, and tonicity.
- ⚡ **[[05 - Cellular Energy MOC|Chapter 5: Cellular Energy MOC]]** — Thermodynamics, enzymes, cellular respiration (glycolysis, Krebs, ETC), and photosynthesis (light reactions, Calvin cycle).
- 🧬 **[[06 - Cell Division MOC|Chapter 6: Cell Division MOC]]** — Mitosis, meiosis, cell cycle regulation, chromosomes, and cancer.

### 🔬 Unit 3: Genetics & Biotechnology
*How genetic information is passed down, expressed at the molecular level, and biotechnology applications.*
- 🎲 **[[07 - Genetics MOC|Chapter 7: Genetics MOC]]** — Mendelian genetics, Punnett squares, non-Mendelian inheritance (codominance, incomplete dominance, sex-linked traits), and pedigree analysis.
- 📜 **[[08 - Molecular Biology MOC|Chapter 8: Molecular Biology MOC]]** — DNA structure, replication, transcription, translation, mutations, and gene regulation.
- 🧬 **[[09 - Biotechnology MOC|Chapter 9: Biotechnology MOC]]** — PCR, gel electrophoresis, DNA sequencing, CRISPR-Cas9, cloning, and recombinant DNA.

### 🦖 Unit 4: Evolution & Ecology
*How life adapts and changes over time, and how organisms interact with each other and their environments.*
- 🦴 **[[10 - Evolution MOC|Chapter 10: Evolution MOC]]** — Natural selection, adaptation, speciation, phylogenetic trees, fossil record, and population genetics.
- 🌍 **[[11 - Ecology MOC|Chapter 11: Ecology MOC]]** — Ecosystems, food webs, trophic levels, population dynamics, ecological succession, and biogeochemical cycles.
- 🌿 **[[16 - Conservation MOC|Chapter 16: Conservation & the Environment MOC]]** — Biodiversity, human impacts, habitat fragmentation, greenhouse effect, and conservation biology.

### 🐕 Unit 5: Diversity & Organismal Biology
*The evolutionary classification of life and the anatomical structures and functions of plants and animals.*
- 🌳 **[[13 - Diversity of Life MOC|Chapter 13: Diversity of Life MOC]]** — Taxonomy, domains and kingdoms, virus characteristics, bacteria, protists, fungi, plants, and animals.
- 🦴 **[[12 - Animal Structure MOC|Chapter 12: Animal Structure & Function MOC]]** — Tissue types, organs, homeostasis, and basic animal anatomy.
- 🌾 **[[14 - Plant Form and Function MOC|Chapter 14: Plant Form and Function MOC]]** — Plant tissues (meristematic, ground, vascular, dermal), xylem/phloem transport, transpiration, and plant hormones.
- 🫁 **[[15 - Human Body Systems MOC|Chapter 15: Human Body Systems MOC]]** — Comprehensive systems coverage including nervous, circulatory, respiratory, digestive, endocrine, and immune systems.

## 📖 Study Guidelines
1. **Explore the MOCs**: Click on a chapter's MOC above to read a high-level **Summary** of the topic and see all the terms defined.
2. **Read the Lessons**: Inside each MOC, click on the **Lesson Page** to read a cohesive narrative that links the individual terms together, acting as a study guide.
3. **Glossary Terms**: The individual notes act as detailed flashcard-like term definitions. Use Obsidian's backlinks and graph view to see how they connect to the rest of the vault!
