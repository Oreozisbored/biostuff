# 🐕 Lesson 12: Animal Anatomy, Physiology, and Homeostasis

Anatomy is the study of an organism's physical structure, while physiology is the study of how those structures function.

---

## 🏗️ Structural Hierarchy in Animals

Animals exhibit a **[[Structural Hierarchy in Animals]]**:
* Cells specialize to form **[[Structural Hierarchy in Animals|Tissues]]**.
* Different tissues group together to form **[[Structural Hierarchy in Animals|Organs]]**.
* Organs cooperate in **[[Organ Systems Overview]]** to perform life functions.

There are four primary tissue types in animals:
1. **[[Epithelial Tissue]]**: Covers the body surface and lines internal organs, protecting, absorbing, and secreting.
2. **[[Connective Tissue]]**: Binds and supports other tissues (e.g., bone, blood, cartilage, adipose tissue).
3. **[[Muscle Tissue]]**: Specialized for contraction and movement. It is divided into:
   * **[[Skeletal Muscle]]**: Voluntary movement (striated, attached to bones).
   * **[[Smooth Muscle]]**: Involuntary movement (non-striated, walls of digestive tract/blood vessels).
   * **[[Cardiac Muscle]]**: Involuntary contraction of the heart (striated, branched, intercalated discs).
4. **[[Nervous Tissue]]**: Senses stimuli and transmits electrical signals. Composed of specialized cells called **[[Neurons]]** and supporting glial cells.

---

## ⚖️ Homeostasis and Feedback Systems

The internal cells of animals are bathed in **[[Interstitial Fluid]]**, which acts as the exchange medium between cells and blood vessels. Animals must keep this internal environment stable, a state of dynamic balance called **[[Homeostasis]]**:
* **[[Negative Feedback]]**: The primary mechanism of homeostasis, where a change in a variable triggers a response that counteracts the change, returning the variable to its set point (e.g., sweating to lower body temperature).
* **[[Positive Feedback]]**: A mechanism that amplifies a change, driving a process to completion (e.g., labor contractions during childbirth, blood clotting).
