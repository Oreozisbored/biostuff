# 🌿 Lesson 16: Conservation Biology and Human Impact

As the human population has grown, our activities have exerted massive pressure on the biosphere. **[[Conservation Biology]]** is a goal-oriented science that seeks to counter the biodiversity crisis.

---

## 🌍 The Dimensions of Biodiversity

We study **[[Biodiversity]]** (biological diversity) at three main levels:
1. **[[Genetic Diversity]]**: The genetic variation within and between populations of a species.
2. **[[Species Diversity]]**: The variety of species in an ecosystem or across the biosphere.
3. **[[Ecosystem Diversity]]**: The variety of ecosystems in the biosphere.

---

## ⚠️ Threats to Biodiversity and Global Change

We are experiencing rapid **[[Global Change]]** due to several human-driven factors. The primary **[[Threats to Biodiversity]]** are:
* **[[Habitat Destruction]]**: The single greatest threat, driven by agriculture, urban sprawl, and forestry, often leading to habitat fragmentation.
* **[[Introduced Species]]**: Non-native species moved by humans to new areas, where they often outcompete native species due to a lack of natural predators.
* **[[Overharvesting]]**: Harvesting wild organisms at rates that exceed the ability of populations to rebound (e.g., overfishing).
* **[[Ozone Depletion]]**: The thinning of the ozone layer due to industrial chemicals (CFCs), increasing harmful UV radiation reaching Earth.
* **[[Climate Change]]**: The long-term shift in global temperatures and weather patterns.
  * This is accelerated by the **[[Greenhouse Effect]]**, where greenhouse gases ($CO_2$, methane) trap solar heat in the atmosphere.

Another major environmental issue is **[[Biomagnification]]**, the concentration of toxins (like heavy metals or pesticides) at higher levels of a food chain.

---

## 🛠️ Solutions: Restoration and Sustainable Development

To combat these threats, ecologists utilize:
* **[[Restoration Ecology]]**: Applying ecological principles to return degraded ecosystems to their natural state.
* **[[Bioremediation]]**: Using living organisms (like plants or microbes) to detoxify polluted areas.
* **[[Protected Areas]]**: Establishing national parks, reserves, and wildlife corridors to protect habitats.
* **[[Ecological Footprint]]**: Measuring the environmental impact of human individuals or populations.
* **[[Sustainable Development]]**: Improving human lives while conserving the ecosystems that support us, securing resources for future generations.
