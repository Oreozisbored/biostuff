# 🌾 Lesson 14: Plant Anatomy, Transport, and Regulation

Plants have evolved unique structural and physiological adaptations to survive as terrestrial autotrophs.

---

## 🏗️ Plant Organs and Tissues

The plant body is organized into three vegetative organs: **[[Roots]]** (anchor and absorb water/nutrients), **[[Stems]]** (support and elevate leaves), and **[[Leaves]]** (the primary sites of photosynthesis).

These organs are composed of three **[[Plant Tissue Systems]]**:
1. **[[Dermal Tissue]]**: The protective outer cover (epidermis).
2. **[[Ground Tissue]]**: Performs photosynthesis, stores food, and provides support.
3. **[[Vascular Tissue]]**: Transports water and nutrients. Composed of:
   * **[[Xylem]]**: Conducts water and dissolved minerals upward from roots.
   * **[[Phloem]]**: Conducts sugars and organic nutrients throughout the plant.

---

## 🔄 Vascular Transport and Transpiration

Water transport in plants is driven by **[[Transpiration]]**, the evaporation of water vapor from leaves:
* Evaporation pulls water up the xylem in a continuous column due to water's cohesive and adhesive properties (the Cohesion-Tension theory).
* Leaves regulate water loss using microscopic pores called **[[Stomata]]**.
* **[[Guard Cells]]** flank each stoma, changing shape to open the pore (to allow carbon dioxide in) or close it (to prevent water loss).

---

## 🌱 Plant Growth and Development

Plants grow throughout their lives due to active division in specialized tissues called **[[Meristems]]**:
* **[[Apical Meristems]]** are located at root and shoot tips, driving **[[Primary Growth]]** (elongation).
* **[[Lateral Meristems]]** drive **[[Secondary Growth]]** (increase in thickness/girth in woody plants). These include:
  * **[[Vascular Cambium]]**: Produces secondary xylem (wood) and secondary phloem.
  * **[[Cork Cambium]]**: Produces the tough outer bark.

---

## 🎲 Reproduction, Seeds, and Fruits

Angiosperms reproduce sexually through flowers:
* **[[Pollination]]**: The transfer of pollen from the male anther to the female stigma.
* **[[Fertilization in Plants]]**: The fusion of sperm and egg inside the ovule.
* **[[Seed Development]]**: The fertilized ovule matures into a seed containing a plant embryo and food supply.
* **[[Fruit Development]]**: The ovary of the flower matures into a fruit, which protects the seed and aids in dispersal.

---

## 🧪 Plant Hormones and Environmental Responses

Plants use chemical messengers called **[[Plant Hormones]]** to regulate growth, development, and responses to stimuli:
* **[[Auxin]]**: Stimulates cell elongation, apical dominance, and phototropism.
* **[[Gibberellins]]**: Promote stem elongation, seed germination, and flowering.
* **[[Cytokinins]]**: Stimulate cell division and delay leaf aging.
* **[[Ethylene]]**: A gas that promotes fruit ripening and leaf drop.
* **[[Abscisic Acid]]** (ABA): Inhibits growth, maintains seed dormancy, and closes stomata during drought.

Plants exhibit growth responses toward or away from stimuli, called tropisms:
* **[[Phototropism]]**: Growth toward light.
* **[[Gravitropism]]**: Growth in response to gravity (roots grow down, shoots grow up).
* **[[Thigmotropism]]**: Growth in response to touch (e.g., vines climbing trellises).
* **[[Photoperiodism]]**: Physiological responses to relative lengths of night and day (regulating seasonal flowering).
