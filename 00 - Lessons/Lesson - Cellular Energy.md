# ⚡ Lesson 5: Bioenergetics, Photosynthesis, and Cellular Respiration

To survive, cells must constantly capture, transform, and utilize energy. These processes are governed by the laws of **[[Thermodynamics]]**:
1. *First Law*: Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transformed.
2. *Second Law*: Every energy transfer increases the entropy (disorder) of the universe.

Biological systems couple energy-releasing reactions with energy-consuming reactions to maintain order.

---

## 🔋 ATP: The Cellular Energy Currency

Cells store and transfer energy using **[[ATP]]** (adenosine triphosphate). ATP is a nucleotide consisting of an adenine base, a ribose sugar, and three phosphate groups:
* The bonds between the phosphate groups are highly unstable and carry negative charges.
* Hydrolysis of the terminal phosphate bond is highly exergonic, releasing energy and forming **[[ADP]]** (adenosine diphosphate) and an inorganic phosphate ($P_i$).
* The cell continuously regenerates ATP by phosphorylating ADP using energy harvested from catabolic pathways.

---

## 🏥 Cellular Respiration: Extracting Energy from Food

Heterotrophs harvest chemical energy from glucose through **[[Cellular Respiration]]**. This catabolic pathway involves multiple stages:

### 1. Glycolysis
Occurs in the cytoplasm and does not require oxygen.
* A glucose molecule (6 carbons) is broken down into two molecules of **[[Glycolysis|Pyruvate]]** (3 carbons).
* This pathway yields a net of 2 ATP and reduces 2 molecules of $NAD^+$ into **[[NAD+ and NADH]]** (high-energy electron carriers).

### 2. Pyruvate Oxidation and the Krebs Cycle
If oxygen is present, pyruvate enters the mitochondrial matrix:
* **[[Pyruvate Oxidation]]**: Pyruvate is oxidized into **[[Pyruvate Oxidation|Acetyl CoA]]**, releasing $CO_2$ and generating NADH.
* **[[Krebs Cycle]]**: Acetyl CoA enters the cycle, where its carbons are fully oxidized to $CO_2$. The cycle yields 2 ATP, 6 NADH, and 2 **[[FAD and FADH2]]** (another electron carrier) per glucose molecule.

### 3. Oxidative Phosphorylation and Chemiosmosis
Occurs on the inner mitochondrial membrane (cristae):
* High-energy electrons from NADH and $FADH_2$ are passed down the **[[Electron Transport Chain]]** (ETC)—a sequence of membrane proteins.
* As electrons flow down the ETC, they lose energy, which is used to pump $H^+$ ions into the intermembrane space, creating a proton gradient.
* Oxygen acts as the final electron acceptor, combining with electrons and protons to form **[[Photosynthesis|Water]]**.
* In a process called **[[Chemiosmosis]]**, the accumulated $H^+$ ions flow back into the matrix through **[[ATP Synthase]]**—a protein channel that acts as a turbine, driving the synthesis of up to 34 ATP per glucose.
* **[[Oxidative Phosphorylation]]** refers to the combination of the ETC and chemiosmosis.

---

## 🍷 Anaerobic Respiration and Fermentation

When oxygen is absent, the electron transport chain halts. To prevent glycolysis from running out of $NAD^+$, cells perform **[[Fermentation]]**:
* **[[Lactic Acid Fermentation]]**: Pyruvate is directly reduced by NADH, forming lactate and regenerating $NAD^+$ (occurs in animal muscles and yogurt-producing bacteria).
* **[[Alcoholic Fermentation]]**: Pyruvate is converted to acetaldehyde (releasing $CO_2$) and then reduced by NADH to ethanol, regenerating $NAD^+$ (occurs in yeast).
* **[[Fermentation|Obligate Anaerobes]]** cannot survive in oxygen; **[[Fermentation|Facultative Anaerobes]]** can toggle between aerobic respiration and fermentation; **[[Fermentation|Obligate Aerobes]]** require oxygen to live.

## ☀️ Photosynthesis: Capturing Solar Energy

Autotrophs use **[[Photosynthesis]]** in their chloroplasts to convert light, water, and $CO_2$ into sugars:

### 1. The Light-Dependent Reactions
Occur in the thylakoid membranes:
* **[[Chlorophyll]]** pigments inside **[[Photosystem II]]** and **[[Photosystem I]]** absorb light, exciting electrons.
* Excited electrons flow down an ETC, generating ATP and **[[Light-Dependent Reactions|NADPH]]**.
* To replace the lost electrons, water is split in a process called **[[Light-Dependent Reactions|Photolysis]]**, releasing oxygen gas ($O_2$) as a byproduct.
* The electrochemical gradient across the **[[Thylakoids]]** drives ATP synthesis.

### 2. The Light-Independent Reactions (Calvin Cycle)
Occurs in the stroma:
* **[[Carbon Fixation]]**: The enzyme **[[RuBisCO]]** fixes atmospheric $CO_2$ to a 5-carbon sugar (RuBP).
* The fixed carbon is reduced using ATP and NADPH from the light reactions, producing a 3-carbon sugar called **[[Calvin Cycle|G3P]]**, which cells use to build glucose.
* The remaining G3P is used to regenerate RuBP.

### 🌵 Photosynthetic Adaptations in Dry Environments
In hot, dry climates, plants close their stomata to prevent water loss, leading to a buildup of $O_2$ and depletion of $CO_2$:
* In **[[Photosynthesis|C3 Plants]]**, this triggers **[[RuBisCO|Photorespiration]]**, a wasteful process where RuBisCO binds $O_2$ instead of $CO_2$.
* **[[Photosynthesis|C4 Plants]]** physically separate carbon fixation from the Calvin cycle, fixing carbon into a 4-carbon compound in bundle-sheath cells.
* **[[Photosynthesis|CAM Plants]]** temporally separate processes; they open stomata only at night, fixing $CO_2$ into organic acids, and release it during the day to power the Calvin cycle while keeping stomata closed.
