# 🫁 Lesson 15: Human Organ Systems and Physiology

The human body coordinates multiple organ systems to maintain homeostasis and carry out life functions.

---

## 🍏 The Digestive and Excretory Systems

The **[[Digestive System]]** processes food, extracts nutrients, and eliminates waste:
* **[[Mechanical Digestion]]**: Physical breakdown of food (chewing in mouth, churning in **[[Stomach]]**).
* **[[Chemical Digestion]]**: Enzymatic breakdown of food.
* **[[Stomach]]**: Secretes acid and pepsin to digest proteins.
* **[[Small Intestine]]**: The primary site of chemical digestion and nutrient absorption.
* **[[Pancreas]]**: Secretes digestive enzymes and bicarbonate buffer into the small intestine.
* **[[Large Intestine]]**: Absorbs water and compacts waste.

The **[[Excretory System]]** removes metabolic wastes and regulates water balance:
* **[[Kidneys]]**: Filter blood to produce urine.
* **[[Nephron]]**: The functional unit of the kidney, which filters blood, reabsorbs nutrients/water, and secretes wastes.

---

## 🫁 The Circulatory and Respiratory Systems

The **[[Circulatory System]]** transports oxygen, nutrients, and wastes throughout the body:
* The **[[Heart]]** acts as a muscular pump.
* **[[Arteries]]** carry oxygenated blood away from the heart.
* **[[Capillaries]]** are tiny vessels where gas and nutrient exchange occurs with tissues.
* **[[Veins]]** return deoxygenated blood back to the heart.
* **[[Blood Component]]s** include plasma, red blood cells (transport $O_2$), white blood cells (fight infection), and platelets (clotting).

The **[[Respiratory System]]** exchanges gases ($O_2$ and $CO_2$) between the body and atmosphere:
* Air passes down the trachea, through bronchi, into the **[[Lungs]]**.
* **[[Alveoli]]** are tiny air sacs in the lungs where gas exchange occurs across thin membranes with capillaries.

---

## 🧠 The Nervous and Endocrine Systems

The **[[Nervous System]]** provides rapid, electrical communication:
* **[[Central Nervous System]]** (CNS): Brain and spinal cord; processes information.
* **[[Peripheral Nervous System]]** (PNS): Nerves throughout the body; transmits signals.

The **[[Endocrine System]]** provides slow, long-lasting chemical communication using **[[Hormones]]** released into the bloodstream:
* **[[Pituitary Gland]]**: The master gland at the base of the brain that regulates other endocrine glands.
* **[[Thyroid Gland]]**: Regulates metabolism.
* **[[Pancreas]]**: Releases insulin and glucagon to regulate blood glucose.

---

## 🛡️ The Immune System

The **[[Immune System]]** defends the body against pathogens:
* **[[Innate Immunity]]**: Non-specific, rapid defenses present from birth (skin, inflammatory response).
* **[[Adaptive Immunity]]**: Specific defenses tailored to individual pathogens, developing memory.
* **[[Antigens]]**: Foreign molecules that trigger an immune response.
* **[[Antibodies]]**: Y-shaped proteins produced by immune cells that bind to and neutralize specific antigens.
* **[[B Cells]]**: Lymphocytes that produce antibodies (humoral response).
* **[[T Cells]]**: Lymphocytes that destroy infected cells (cell-mediated response).

*Note: The human body also utilizes a **[[Reproductive System]]** to produce gametes and support offspring development.*
